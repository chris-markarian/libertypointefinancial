What makes this strategy different

🚫 Downside protection built in

Your IUL cash value is linked to market index performance — but a guaranteed 0% floor means your cash value is protected from market losses. You participate in gains, not the losses.

💰 Significant tax advantages

IUL cash value grows tax-deferred, and policy loans are generally income tax-free when the policy is properly structured and maintained. Consult your tax advisor for your specific situation.

📈 No IRS contribution limits

Already maxed your 401(k)? IUL policies have no IRS contribution caps — making them a popular supplemental strategy for high earners who want additional tax-advantaged savings.

🛡️ Living benefits included

Many IUL policies include accelerated death benefit riders for critical, chronic, and terminal illness — allowing you to access a portion of the death benefit while living if you qualify.

🏦 Flexible access to cash value

Access your accumulated cash value through policy loans or withdrawals. Loans are generally not subject to income tax, though unpaid loans reduce the death benefit. No age restrictions or early withdrawal penalties like a 401(k).

👨‍👩‍👧 Income tax-free death benefit

Your beneficiaries receive a death benefit that is generally income tax-free under IRC 7702. It's life insurance that also functions as a powerful long-term financial planning tool.

How It Works

The three components of an IUL policy

An Indexed Universal Life policy isn't just life insurance — it's a multi-purpose financial tool. Here's how the pieces fit together.

🛡️

Death Benefit

A permanent, income tax-free death benefit paid to your beneficiaries. Unlike term insurance, it never expires as long as the policy is properly funded.

📈

Cash Value

A cash value account that grows tax-deferred, linked to a market index like the S&P 500. Gains are capped, but a 0% floor protects you from market losses.

❤️

Living Benefits

Accelerated benefit riders that allow you to access a portion of the death benefit early if diagnosed with a critical, chronic, or terminal illness.
