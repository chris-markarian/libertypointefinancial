What makes this strategy different

🚫 Downside protection built in

Your IUL cash value is linked to market index performance — but a guaranteed 0% floor means your cash value is protected from market losses. You participate in gains, not the losses.

💰 Significant tax advantages

IUL cash value grows tax-deferred, and policy loans are generally income tax-free when the policy is properly structured and maintained. Consult your tax advisor for your specific situation.

📈 No IRS contribution limits

Already maxed your 401(k)? IUL policies have no IRS contribution caps — making them a popular supplemental strategy for high earners who want additional tax-advantaged savings.

🛡️ Living benefits included

Many IUL policies include accelerated death benefit riders for critical, chronic, and terminal illness — allowing you to access a portion of the death benefit while living if you qualify.

🏦 Flexible access to cash value

Access your accumulated cash value through policy loans or withdrawals. Loans are generally not subject to income tax, though unpaid loans reduce the death benefit. No age restrictions or early withdrawal penalties like a 401(k).

👨‍👩‍👧 Income tax-free death benefit

Your beneficiaries receive a death benefit that is generally income tax-free under IRC 7702. It's life insurance that also functions as a powerful long-term financial planning tool.

How It Works

The three components of an IUL policy

An Indexed Universal Life policy isn't just life insurance — it's a multi-purpose financial tool. Here's how the pieces fit together.

🛡️

Death Benefit

A permanent, income tax-free death benefit paid to your beneficiaries. Unlike term insurance, it never expires as long as the policy is properly funded.

📈

Cash Value

A cash value account that grows tax-deferred, linked to a market index like the S&P 500. Gains are capped, but a 0% floor protects you from market losses.

❤️

Living Benefits

Accelerated benefit riders that allow you to access a portion of the death benefit early if diagnosed with a critical, chronic, or terminal illness.

One Policy. Built Around Your Life.

An IUL can be many things — depending on what you need it to be

Most financial products do one thing. An Indexed Universal Life policy can be structured and customized to solve very different problems for very different people. Here are just some of the ways it's being used right now.

✓

A supplement to your 401(k) or IRA

Already maxing out your qualified accounts? IUL has no IRS contribution limits and offers tax-advantaged growth with generally tax-free access — making it a powerful complement to any retirement strategy.

✓

A death benefit that protects your family

If something happens to you prematurely, your beneficiaries receive a permanent, income tax-free death benefit — providing financial security when it's needed most.

✓

A safe place to grow and park money

With a 0% floor and tax-deferred growth, an IUL is a low-risk vehicle to accumulate cash value — while keeping it accessible through policy loans whenever you need it.

✓

A living benefits safety net

Many IUL policies include accelerated benefit riders that allow you to access part of your death benefit if you're diagnosed with a critical illness (heart attack, stroke, cancer), chronic illness, or terminal illness — while you're still alive.

✓

A maximum accumulation vehicle

For clients focused purely on growth, an IUL can be structured with a minimum death benefit and maximum premium — directing as much as possible into the cash value for long-term tax-advantaged accumulation.

✓

A strategy to pay off your mortgage early

By leveraging the participating loan feature, some policyholders use IUL cash value to systematically eliminate their mortgage years ahead of schedule — while their cash value continues earning index credits on the full balance.

✓

A tool for the middle class and the wealthy alike

For middle-income earners, it provides protection and a tax-advantaged retirement supplement. For high earners, it becomes a powerful tax mitigation strategy. For the ultra-wealthy, it's an estate planning and wealth transfer vehicle. The structure just looks different.

✓

A business planning tool

Business owners use IUL for key-person insurance, executive bonus arrangements, and buy-sell agreement funding — protecting the business while building tax-advantaged cash value for the owner.

No two IUL policies look exactly alike. That's the point. The right policy is engineered around your goals, your situation, and your timeline — not off a shelf. That's what a strategy conversation with Liberty Pointe Financial is designed to figure out.
